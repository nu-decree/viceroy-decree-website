This page is a companion to the **[Course Curricula](/course-curricula/)**. It lists the **[DoD Cyber Workforce Framework (DCWF)](https://dodcio.defense.gov/Cyber-Workforce/DCWF/)** workforce elements and work roles that the curriculum maps to, plus the full DCWF catalog for reference.

The DCWF, defined in **DoDD 8140.01** and operationalized through **DoDM 8140.03**, is the DoD's authoritative lexicon of cyber work. It builds on the **[NICE Workforce Framework for Cybersecurity](https://www.nist.gov/itl/applied-cybersecurity/nice/nice-framework-resource-center)** and organizes work into **seven workforce elements** and **74 work roles**, each with a unique three-digit code, a definition, and a set of tasks and knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs). A short official overview is in the **[DCWF Overview Factsheet (PDF)](https://dl.dod.cyber.mil/wp-content/uploads/dcwf/pdf/unclass-dcwf_factsheet.pdf)**.

Each work role name links to a public detail page: the **SANS job-role page** for that DCWF code where one exists (role summary plus aligned training), otherwise the official **DoD DCWF work role page**. Roles marked **★** are mapped in the VICEROY DECREE curriculum.

Definitions below are quoted from the official DCWF work role pages.

---
## Workforce Elements

| Element | What the element covers |
| ------- | ----------------------- |
| **IT (Cyberspace)** | Personnel who design, build, configure, operate, and maintain IT, networks, and capabilities, including information resource management and the management, storage, transmission, and display of data. |
| **Cybersecurity** | Personnel who secure, defend, and preserve data, networks, and systems by ensuring appropriate security controls are in place and taking internal defense actions. |
| **Software Engineering** | Personnel who design, develop, test, secure, and sustain software and software-intensive systems across the full development lifecycle. |
| **Cyberspace Effects** | Personnel who plan, support, and execute cyberspace capabilities to externally defend or conduct force projection in or through cyberspace. |
| **Intelligence (Cyberspace)** | Personnel who collect, process, analyze, and disseminate intelligence on foreign actors' cyberspace programs, capabilities, and operational activities. |
| **AI / Data** | Personnel who engineer, manage, analyze, and govern data and artificial intelligence / machine learning capabilities. |
| **Cyberspace Enablers (Support)** | Personnel who support or facilitate the cyber workforce through acquisition, legal / law enforcement, training, and leadership functions. |

| 622 ★ | [Secure Software Assessor](https://www.sans.org/job-roles/secure-software-assessor-dcwf-622) | Analyzes the security of new or existing computer applications, software, or specialized utility programs and provides actionable results. |
